CP-009.1

5 Chinese Brands Just Made the Paris Fashion Week Calendar. And Margiela Picked Shanghai Over Paris.

Read that headline again. Five Chinese fashion brands on the official Paris Fashion Week FW2026 schedule. Not as guests. Not in off-calendar pop-ups. On the actual programme, alongside Chanel, Dior, and Louis Vuitton.

And then there’s Maison Margiela, one of fashion’s most cult European houses, choosing to skip Paris entirely and debut its FW2026 collection in Shanghai instead.

If you still think “Chinese fashion” means factory floors and copycat streetwear, March 2026 just proved you dead wrong.

The Five Chinese Brands on the Paris Calendar

Paris Fashion Week ran March 2-10, 2026, with 98 shows across 67 runway presentations and 31 static exhibitions. Among them, five Chinese brands earned official slots:

Uma Wang (王汁) — March 5

Shanghai-based designer known for poetic, layered silhouettes that blur Eastern philosophy and Western tailoring.

ICICLE (之禾) — March 5

A major Chinese womenswear brand with serious commercial scale, proving this moment is not only indie and niche.

Shiatzy Chen (夏姿陈) — March 9

A Taiwanese-Chinese house blending Eastern craft with haute couture sensibilities and long-running Paris relevance.

Reverie by Caroline Hu (胡颖琪) — March 4

Dreamy, tulle-heavy work from a young globally fluent designer whose references remain unapologetically Chinese.

Ruohan (晏若涵) — March 6

A rising name representing designers who see no contradiction between Chinese identity and global ambition.

Five brands. Five different aesthetics. One shared signal: Chinese design has graduated from “emerging” to “expected” on the world’s most exclusive runway calendar.

Margiela’s Shanghai Pivot Is the Bigger Story

Maison Margiela didn’t just skip the Paris calendar this season. The house announced its FW2026 global premiere will happen in Shanghai on April 1 as the headline event of Shanghai Fashion Week.

This is the first time Margiela has moved a core collection show to China. Not a capsule preview. Not a local activation. The actual global debut.

CEO Gaetano Sciuto called it “an important signal of the brand deepening its roots in the Chinese market,” framing Shanghai as a city that retains tradition while continuously blending with modern elements.

But the deeper logic is customer gravity. Margiela is moving where high-intent luxury demand already sits, then extending the show with a four-city public program bookable through WeChat mini-programs.

What This Power Shift Actually Means

Let’s zoom out. In a single fashion season, you have:

Chinese Brands Going to Paris

  • Five official slots across both independent designers and commercial brands.
  • Chinese celebrities dominating front rows from Loewe to Dior, Givenchy, Celine, and Vivienne Westwood.

European Brands Coming to Shanghai

  • Maison Margiela choosing Shanghai over Paris for its global debut.
  • Valentino also skipping Paris for a Rome show, confirming traffic is no longer one-way.

The traffic isn’t just moving in one direction anymore. Chinese brands are going to Paris and European brands are coming to Shanghai. Fashion’s center of gravity isn’t shifting so much as splitting.

For Western brands, this rewrites assumptions about where cultural authority lives. If Margiela decides Shanghai is worth more than Paris for a global debut, the signal is bigger than any single show.

CP-009.2

Born on Taobao. Raised on Xiaohongshu. Now Sitting Next to Louis Vuitton.

A brand that started selling clothes on Taobao just opened a store at China’s highest-grossing shopping mall. Not in the basement. Not in the food court. On the premium floors. Next to the luxury houses.

This isn’t one brand getting lucky. It’s a pattern. And if you’re a Western fashion brand planning your China retail strategy, the game just changed under your feet.

The Mall That Only Lets Winners In

Nanjing Deji Plaza is China’s “store king,” with 24.5 billion yuan in annual sales. In 2025 and early 2026, three brands born on Taobao entered the building:

Brand Origin Deji Plaza Move Total Stores
CHICJOC Taobao Opened at Deji Plaza plus Paris and Los Angeles 41
Unica Taobao / Xiaohongshu First offline store at Deji Plaza 1
Marius Taobao First offline store at Deji Plaza 1

CHICJOC is the most aggressive of the three. Forty-one stores and counting, with locations in Paris and Los Angeles alongside its Deji Plaza presence. That’s a Taobao brand with international retail in 2026.

kungfudata.com 8

The Secret Weapon: Same Fabrics, a Fraction of the Price

These brands are sourcing from the same fabric suppliers and manufacturers as the luxury houses. The difference is structural, not mystical.

The Cost Advantages

  • No Paris showroom rent
  • No global marketing campaigns
  • No wholesale middlemen
  • No legacy overhead

The Result: Their Price Range

  • Basics and daily wear: under 1,000 yuan ($137)
  • Mid-range pieces: 1,000 to 3,000 yuan
  • Premium items: up to 10,000+ yuan ($1,370)

For a Deji Plaza shopper, the math is simple: spend 30,000 yuan on a luxury jacket, or spend 3,000 yuan next door on comparable fabric and increasingly comparable quality. The logo is different. The quality gap is shrinking fast.

KEIGAN: The One That Went Full Luxury

If CHICJOC represents Taobao brands moving up, KEIGAN represents something bolder. The brand opened “MAISON KEIGAN 1490” on Shanghai’s Nanjing West Road, right next to Prada Rongzhai, one of Asia’s most iconic luxury retail spaces.

The name tells you everything. “Maison” is a word reserved for fashion houses. “1490” references the street address. This is not a store opening. It is a claim to category status.

KEIGAN is positioning as a Chinese maison with atelier-level pricing and appointment-only shopping for its highest-tier collections. In other words, it is not trying to be premium fast fashion. It is trying to be a house.

Why This Is Happening Now

Three forces are colliding to make this moment possible.

01

Chinese consumers trust Chinese brands now

Guochao has matured beyond simple patriotism. Consumers have compared fabrics, read Xiaohongshu reviews, and stopped assuming European labels are automatically better.

02

Malls need traffic, and these brands bring it

CHICJOC and peers bring massive digital followings that convert into physical visits, a demographic premium malls increasingly struggle to win through legacy luxury alone.

03

Fast fashion left a vacuum

As ZARA and H&M pull back, premium space opens up. Mall operators are replacing them with domestic brands that sit between fast fashion and luxury.

What This Means for Western Brands

It’s not just the luxury houses above you and the fast-fashion chains below you. It’s a new middle tier of Chinese brands that were born digital and now show up in physical spaces you assumed required a 50-year history.

01

Your fabric advantage is gone.

“Made in Italy” still carries weight, but “same fabric, same Chinese factory” is a harder story to beat.

02

Instagram followers do not equal trust inside Taobao, Xiaohongshu, and Douyin ecosystems.

CP-009.3

Victoria’s Secret Just Closed Its Biggest China Store After 9 Years. Here’s Why That’s Actually Good News.

2,000 square meters. Nine years of operation. Shanghai’s Lipo Plaza on Huaihai Road. Victoria’s Secret just shut the doors on the flagship that launched its entire China chapter.

And yet, this might be the smartest move the brand has made in China since it arrived.

kungfudata.com 13

What Happened

Victoria’s Secret closed its first and largest full-category flagship in mainland China, the 2017 Lipo Plaza location that anchored the brand’s early China expansion.

The Old Chapter

Opened in 2017 on Huaihai Road, housing full-range lingerie, beauty, and experiential marketing under one roof.

The New Chapter

Chinese media report that VS plans a new flagship at “Huaihai 755,” also on the same street, as early as September 2026. This looks like relocation, not retreat.

The brand isn’t shrinking in China. It’s shedding expensive legacy real estate to go lighter, more digital, and more local.

The Yang Mi Play

Victoria’s Secret signed Yang Mi and Tian Xiwei as Chinese brand ambassadors. Yang Mi alone brings the kind of cultural gravity that moves product overnight through content, not square footage.

The Old Model

Giant flagship stores, angel-wing photo ops, expensive square footage, and passive brand presence.

The New Model

Entertainment-driven content, social storytelling, and digital-first brand building on Xiaohongshu and Douyin.

This is not a billboard celebrity play. It is a distribution strategy built around platform-native entertainment and the kind of attention architecture that reaches millions without renting a single extra square meter.

Why This Matters: The 320 Billion RMB Market

China’s innerwear market is worth roughly 320 billion RMB ($44 billion), and it is being rewired around comfort, digital distribution, and content-led attention.

01

Comfort over sexy

Wireless, seamless, and athleisure-style innerwear has replaced the old push-up default. VS adapted with comfort-first products like the anti-gravity bra.

02

Digital-first distribution

The Virgini joint venture restructured China specifically for e-commerce and digital marketing, with a 49% stake acquired for $45 million.

03

Content, not stores, builds brands now

Brand heat comes from livestreams, outfit posts, and celebrity-driven content that can reach 100 million eyeballs without paying rent.

Most Western brands that close stores in China are circling the drain. The distinction here is that Victoria’s Secret is closing one expensive flagship while the underlying business is growing.

Victoria’s Secret Is Different

Revenue is up. Innerwear is up for the first time in four years. PINK is growing. Beauty is approaching a billion dollars. That makes the store closure look like portfolio discipline, not collapse.

The deeper difference is operational. VS rebuilt from the ground up, brought in a local joint venture partner that understands Chinese e-commerce, signed the right celebrities, and shifted product mix to match what Chinese consumers actually want.
