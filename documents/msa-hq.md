# Master Services Agreement (MSA)

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of **[Effective Date]** ("Effective Date") by and between:

- **Kung Fu Data Pte. Ltd.**, a company organized under the laws of Singapore, with registered office at [Insert Address], Singapore ("Client" or "KFD"); and
- **Auvihaus Global Private Limited**, a company organized under the laws of India, CIN U73100WR2026PTC292308, with registered office at HILAND GREEN PHASE 1, BL-7, 12TH FLR, FL-A5, Batanagar, Bishnupur - I, South 24 Parganas- 700140, Kolkata, India ("Service Provider" or "Auvihaus").

Client and Service Provider are each a "Party" and together the "Parties."

## 1. Purpose and Scope

1.1 This Agreement sets out the legal, commercial, governance, intellectual property, continuity, confidentiality, risk allocation, and dispute-resolution framework governing services performed by Service Provider for Client.

1.2 Specific services, milestones, deliverables, timelines, acceptance criteria, and fees are set out in one or more Statements of Work and/or Order Forms (each, an "SOW") executed by both Parties.

1.3 This Agreement applies to all SOWs executed under it unless an SOW expressly states otherwise.

1.4 This Agreement is a master legal framework and is not a substitute for a detailed SOW. Feature-level inclusions, exclusions, and delivery specifics are agreed in the applicable SOW before legal execution of delivery phases.

1.5 On reasonable written request for diligence, each Party will provide basic corporate identity and authority information relevant to contract execution (for example, registration details and authorized signatory authority), subject to law and confidentiality obligations.

## 2. Order of Precedence and Related Agreements

2.1 The following order of precedence applies in case of conflict:

- This Agreement controls legal and commercial terms, including liability, intellectual property, confidentiality, dispute resolution, and continuity rights.
- The applicable SOW controls service scope, delivery schedules, operational milestones, acceptance criteria, and pricing mechanics, except where this Agreement expressly overrides.

2.2 The Parties acknowledge an existing NDA between them (the "NDA"). The NDA is incorporated by reference. If this Agreement and the NDA differ, the provision that provides greater protection to Confidential Information governs. Otherwise, both apply cumulatively.

## 3. Term and Renewal

3.1 This Agreement starts on the Effective Date and remains in force until terminated in accordance with Section 17.

3.2 Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, services continue on a rolling monthly basis.

## 4. Commercial Model, Fees, and Billing Trigger

4.1 The Parties agree that commercial terms may be structured in multiple parts (including, where applicable, separate fee tracks for Business HQ and personal-agent services), as detailed in the applicable SOW.

4.2 Unless otherwise stated in the SOW:

- Invoices are issued monthly in arrears for active recurring services.
- Payment term is Net 15 calendar days from invoice date.

4.3 For the current Business HQ engagement, recurring monthly HQ fees begin only after Client written sign-off of Phase 1 readiness as defined in the SOW.

4.4 Any personal-agent fee track (including setup and recurring charges) starts only when that personal-agent phase formally commences under the SOW's phase-gating and timing conditions.

4.5 Disputed invoice amounts must be raised in writing in good faith within 10 business days of invoice receipt. Undisputed amounts remain payable by the due date.

4.6 Unless expressly stated in an SOW, this Agreement does not impose minimum-volume purchase commitments or rigid lock-in commitments.

## 5. Service Governance

5.1 The Parties will operate under the governance cadence defined in the SOW, including review sessions, feedback loops, escalation channels, and acceptance workflows.

5.2 The "execution-first" operating model is permitted only within approved safety and approval controls defined in the SOW and Exhibit D.

5.3 Client remains final decision-maker for sensitive business actions requiring human approval under the SOW.

5.4 The Parties acknowledge a retained-services model, not a software-landlord model. Service Provider is responsible for professional service delivery under the SOW and this Agreement, and Client retains operational control rights described herein.

5.5 Service design may include multi-model and multi-provider routing (for example, OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and other providers) where specified in the SOW; nothing in this Agreement requires single-provider lock-in unless explicitly agreed in writing.

5.6 The Parties may modify operational scope and priorities through signed SOW amendments or change orders without replacing this Agreement.

## 7. Intellectual Property Ownership and Licenses

7.1 **Service Provider Background IP.** Service Provider retains all right, title, and interest in and to Service Provider Background IP, including orchestration frameworks, reusable modules, generic methods, non-client templates, tools, and platform know-how.

7.2 **Client IP.** Client owns all right, title, and interest in and to Client IP, including Client Data, client-specific business rules, client-specific workflow configurations, client-specific prompt packs, client outputs, and deliverables expressly designated as Client work product in an SOW.

7.3 **Improvements.**

- Generic improvements, techniques, and platform-level enhancements that are not Client-specific are Service Provider Background IP.
- Client-specific embodiments, configurations, and logic implemented uniquely for Client are Client IP.

7.4 **License to Client.** Service Provider grants Client a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free license (including right to use, run, copy, modify, maintain, and authorize third-party maintainers) to all Client IP and any necessary Service Provider interfaces embedded in the delivered solution solely to operate, maintain, and extend Client's deployment in Client-controlled environments.

7.5 **License to Service Provider.** Client grants Service Provider a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use Client Confidential Information solely to perform services under this Agreement and applicable SOWs.

7.6 **No Implied Transfer.** No ownership transfer occurs except as expressly stated in this Agreement.

7.7 **No "Landlord" Restriction on Continuity.** Service Provider shall not withhold, revoke, or condition Client's continuity rights to Client-owned assets and deployment-operational materials on the basis of business model characterization. Client continuity rights under Sections 7 and 8 survive termination as stated in this Agreement.

## 8. Continuity, Step-In Rights, and Operational Independence

8.1 Production environments for the contracted system will be established and maintained under Client-controlled accounts and infrastructure as described in the SOW and Exhibit B.

8.2 Service Provider must maintain and deliver the Continuity Access Pack described in Exhibit B, including architecture, runbooks, configuration inventories, credential-rotation maps, deployment restore procedures, and current client-specific configuration snapshots.

8.3 **Step-In Trigger Events.** Client step-in rights activate automatically if Service Provider:

- enters insolvency, administration, liquidation, or equivalent proceedings;
- ceases operations; or
- is unable to provide contracted services for 30 consecutive days for reasons not caused by Client.

8.4 Upon a Step-In Trigger Event, Client has immediate rights to continue operations using Client-controlled environments and delivered continuity materials, and may appoint a replacement provider.

8.5 Service Provider will cooperate in good faith during transition and provide reasonable technical assistance for continuity, subject to pre-agreed charges for out-of-scope support under Section 18.

8.6 Service Provider will not unreasonably block continuity access to materials required to operate the active Client deployment in Client-controlled environments.

## 9. Confidentiality and Data Protection

9.1 Each Party shall protect the other Party's Confidential Information using at least reasonable care and no less than the standard used to protect its own information of similar sensitivity.

9.2 Confidential Information may be used only for performing or receiving services under this Agreement.

9.3 Neither Party may disclose Confidential Information except:

- to personnel/contractors who need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective; or
- where required by law, with prior notice where legally permitted.

9.4 Confidentiality obligations survive for 5 years after termination of this Agreement. Trade secrets and source-like materials remain protected for as long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.

9.5 Without limiting the above, Service Provider shall not disclose Client-specific workflows, prompt packs, logic maps, strategy artifacts, or other client-specific operational designs except as expressly authorized in writing by Client.

## 10. Restricted Reuse, Resale, and Competitor Controls

10.1 Service Provider shall not resell, disclose, license, assign, or reuse Client-Specific Workflow Assets for any third party.

10.2 In addition to Section 10.1, Service Provider shall not deploy Materially Similar Workflow Implementations to a Direct China-Region Competitor for 24 months from first production deployment of the relevant Client-Specific Workflow Asset, unless Client gives prior written consent.

10.3 Direct China-Region Competitor determination shall follow Exhibit C:

- Named competitor list maintained by Client and updated quarterly; and
- objective criteria for unnamed entities.

10.4 Generalized, non-identifying know-how that does not disclose or replicate Client-Specific Workflow Assets may be used by Service Provider, subject to confidentiality and competition restrictions in this Agreement.

## 11. Data Rights, Portability, and Training Restrictions

11.1 Client owns all Client Data and all client outputs generated for Client operations under this Agreement.

11.2 Service Provider shall not use Client Confidential Information or Client-Specific Workflow Assets to train, market, package, or commercialize client-specific offerings for others.

11.3 On termination or on Client written request during term, Service Provider shall provide a current export of Client-owned data and client-specific workflow artifacts in reasonably portable formats (including, where applicable, JSON/CSV/Markdown/YAML and configuration documentation) within 10 business days unless otherwise specified in the SOW.

11.4 Service Provider may retain minimal archival copies only as required by law, provided such retained data remains subject to confidentiality obligations.

## 12. Representations, Warranties, and Disclaimers

12.1 Each Party represents that:

- it is duly organized and validly existing under applicable law;
- it has full authority to enter this Agreement; and
- execution and performance do not violate binding obligations.

12.2 Service Provider warrants that services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards.

12.3 AI systems and model outputs are probabilistic; Service Provider does not warrant perfect accuracy of every output. This disclaimer does not remove Service Provider's obligations to perform contracted services, maintain agreed controls, and remediate material functional failures under the SOW.

## 13. Indemnities

13.1 **Service Provider Indemnity.** Service Provider shall defend and indemnify Client from third-party claims alleging that Service Provider-supplied materials infringe intellectual property rights, except to the extent claims arise from Client-provided materials, unauthorized modifications, or combinations not directed by Service Provider.

13.2 If an infringement claim occurs, Service Provider may, at its option and expense:

- procure rights for continued use;
- replace or modify the relevant component to be non-infringing; or
- if neither is commercially reasonable, terminate the affected component and refund prepaid unused fees for that component.

13.3 **Client Indemnity.** Client shall defend and indemnify Service Provider from third-party claims arising from Client-provided unlawful data, instructions, content, or distributions controlled by Client.

13.4 **Claim Process Controls.** The indemnified Party must promptly notify the indemnifying Party of claims, provide reasonable cooperation, and allow the indemnifying Party control of defense and settlement, except no settlement may impose non-monetary obligations or admissions on the indemnified Party without written consent.

## 14. Liability Cap and Exclusions

14.1 Except for carve-outs in Section 14.3, each Party's aggregate liability arising out of or related to this Agreement is limited to the total fees paid or payable under this Agreement and applicable SOWs in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to liability.

14.2 Neither Party is liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including loss of profits, revenue, goodwill, or anticipated savings, except where such exclusion is prohibited by law.

14.3 The liability cap in Section 14.1 does not apply to:

- a Party's confidentiality breach obligations under Section 9;
- a Party's indemnity obligations under Section 13;
- willful misconduct or fraud; or
- Client payment obligations for undisputed fees.

## 15. Compliance, Security, and Safety Gates

15.1 Each Party shall comply with applicable laws in performing obligations under this Agreement.

15.2 Security baseline, approval gate responsibilities, and support baseline are set out in Exhibit D.

15.3 Service Provider will not knowingly execute prohibited actions that violate applicable laws or platform terms, and may pause execution pending clarification where necessary.

## 16. Publicity, Non-Solicit, and Assignment

16.1 Neither Party may use the other Party's name, trademarks, or logos in external publicity without prior written consent.

16.2 During term and for 12 months after termination, neither Party shall knowingly solicit for employment the other Party's personnel materially involved in services under this Agreement, except through general public recruiting not targeted at such personnel.

16.3 Either Party may assign this Agreement in connection with merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets, provided the assignee assumes all obligations in writing and prompt notice is provided to the other Party.

## 17. Termination

17.1 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or an active SOW for convenience by giving 30 days' written notice.

17.2 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate for material breach if the breach is not cured within 15 days after written notice.

17.3 **Immediate Termination.** Either Party may terminate immediately on insolvency, dissolution, or cessation of operations of the other Party.

17.4 Termination does not affect accrued payment obligations for services performed through the effective termination date.

17.5 Surviving provisions include payment obligations, intellectual property, confidentiality, indemnities, liability limits/exclusions, continuity and transition obligations, and dispute resolution.

## 18. Exit Transition Assistance

18.1 On termination or expiration, Service Provider shall provide transition assistance and continuity cooperation consistent with this Agreement, Exhibit B, and the SOW handover obligations.

18.2 Required baseline transition deliverables are included as part of contracted services where specified in SOW.

18.3 Any additional transition support beyond contracted baseline is billable only if pre-agreed in writing, with scope, rates, and timeline documented.

## 19. Dispute Resolution and Governing Law

19.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of Singapore, excluding conflict-of-law rules.

19.2 Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, including existence, validity, interpretation, performance, breach, or termination, shall be finally resolved by arbitration administered by the Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC) under SIAC Rules in force when the Notice of Arbitration is submitted.

19.3 Seat of arbitration is Singapore. Tribunal shall consist of one arbitrator unless SIAC Rules require otherwise. Proceedings shall be in English.

19.4 Nothing in this Agreement prevents either Party from seeking interim or injunctive relief from a court of competent jurisdiction for confidentiality, data protection, or intellectual property breaches.

## 20. General Provisions

20.1 **Notices.** Formal notices shall be in writing to addresses/emails designated by each Party in signature block or later written notice.

20.2 **Force Majeure.** Neither Party is liable for delay/failure caused by events beyond reasonable control, except payment obligations for services already performed.

20.3 **Independent Contractors.** Parties are independent contractors; nothing creates partnership, joint venture, employment, or agency.

20.4 **Severability.** If any provision is invalid or unenforceable, remaining provisions continue in full force.

20.5 **Waiver.** Failure to enforce a provision is not a waiver unless in writing.

20.6 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, exhibits, and executed SOWs constitute the entire agreement on subject matter and supersede prior discussions on that subject matter.

20.7 **Amendments.** Amendments must be in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

20.8 **Counterparts and E-Signature.** This Agreement may be signed in counterparts and via electronic signature, each deemed an original.

## Exhibit A - Definitions and IP Taxonomy

### A.1 Key Definitions

- **"Background IP"** means intellectual property owned or controlled by a Party before the Effective Date, or developed independently of this Agreement.
- **"Client Data"** means all data, files, records, and information provided by or for Client, and data generated from Client operations.
- **"Client IP"** means Client Data, Client-specific business rules, Client-specific workflow configurations, Client-specific prompt packs, Client outputs, and deliverables designated as Client work product in an SOW.
- **"Client-Specific Workflow Assets"** means workflow architecture, logic, prompts, playbooks, configuration maps, scoring frameworks, outreach logic, and operational artifacts uniquely configured for Client.
- **"Confidential Information"** means non-public information disclosed by a Party that is marked confidential or reasonably understood as confidential.
- **"Materially Similar Workflow Implementation"** means a workflow that replicates substantial functional logic, sequencing, decision criteria, and/or proprietary strategy profile of Client-Specific Workflow Assets such that it would reasonably be viewed as a substitute implementation of Client's unique operational design.
- **"Direct China-Region Competitor"** means an entity identified under Exhibit C as competing directly in relevant products/services and commercial operations in the China region.

### A.2 IP Taxonomy Matrix

| Asset Category | Ownership | Usage Rights |
|---|---|---|
| Service Provider orchestration framework, reusable tools, generic libraries, platform know-how | Service Provider | Client receives rights only as needed to run Client deployment under this Agreement |
| Client data, client prompts, client business rules, client workflow configurations, client outputs | Client | Service Provider may use only to perform services |
| Generic improvements not specific to Client | Service Provider | Client may benefit through service delivery |
| Client-specific embodiments/custom workflow logic for Client use | Client | Client may operate/modify/maintain in Client-controlled environments |

### A.3 No Inference Rule

No right to use or commercialize the other Party's owned assets exists unless expressly granted by this Agreement.

## Exhibit B - Continuity and Handover Access Pack Requirements

### B.1 Client-Controlled Infrastructure Principle

Production deployment shall be in Client-controlled accounts/environments unless expressly approved otherwise in writing by Client.

### B.2 Mandatory Continuity Access Pack

Service Provider shall maintain and provide the following current items:

- System overview and component map.
- Architecture and environment diagrams.
- Data flow map.
- Configuration inventory and environment variable location map.
- Secret references and credential-rotation playbook (without exposing plaintext secrets outside secure channels).
- Workflow runbooks for active workflows.
- Incident and failure-mode handling guide.
- Deployment, backup, and restore procedures.
- Current snapshot of Client-specific prompt/workflow logic.
- Role/access matrix and access revocation checklist.

### B.3 Delivery Timing

- During active engagement: on reasonable Client request, and at minimum quarterly refresh.
- On termination notice: final pack delivered no later than final business day of notice period, subject to required environment access.

### B.4 Step-In Cooperation

Upon Step-In Trigger Event, Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition cooperation to enable uninterrupted operation by Client or replacement provider.

### B.5 Continuity Verification Checklist

At handover, Service Provider shall provide written confirmation that:

- Infrastructure remains under Client control.
- Operational access is held by Client administrators.
- Service Provider access can be revoked cleanly.
- Credential-rotation steps are documented.
- Active workflows can run without Service Provider-controlled accounts.

- Role-based access controls for operational tooling.

### D.3 Execution Boundaries

### D.4 Support Baseline

- Response and coordination cadence follows SOW.
- Material functional blockers receive priority remediation.
- Iterative tuning and calibration are performed per active SOW commitments.
