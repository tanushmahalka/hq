## Design Context

### Users
HQ is for business operators and owners, especially non-technical leaders running AI-enabled operations at meaningful scale. The primary mental model is a CEO running a roughly $20M agency who uses the product throughout the day to supervise, coordinate, and control AI agents without getting dragged into implementation detail.

The product should help users automate large parts of their business with AI while still feeling firmly in control. The interface should support scanning over studying, quick decisions over deep configuration, and operational confidence over technical complexity.

### Brand Personality
The emotional target is authority, confidence, and control, delivered with calmness. Users should feel that the system is powerful, reliable, and already working on their behalf.

The brand promise can be summarized as: automate your whole business with AI.

The interface should feel appropriate for a serious executive audience. It should avoid anything that feels juvenile, flashy, gimmicky, toy-like, hacky, or trend-chasing. If a design choice would make a $20M CEO hesitate to trust the product, it is the wrong choice.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode is the primary experience and should receive the most design attention. The current warm-neutral system is the right foundation: cream-toned backgrounds, restrained contrast, precise typography, and violet used as an intelligent accent rather than decorative noise.

The visual tone should be polished, composed, and executive. It should feel like a premium operating surface for business automation: spacious, legible, warm, and decisive. Motion should remain subtle and purposeful. Surfaces should communicate hierarchy through tone, border discipline, and rhythm rather than heavy shadow or loud color blocking.

There are no explicit reference products to mimic, so design decisions should stay grounded in the repo's existing system rather than chasing external aesthetics. Anti-reference guidance is simple: do not make this look like a startup toy, a gamer dashboard, a crypto product, or a generic AI demo.

### Design Principles
1. Design for executive scanability. Prioritize clarity, hierarchy, and at-a-glance comprehension over density or novelty.
2. Make automation feel controlled. AI should appear competent and active, but never chaotic, mysterious, or overwhelming.
3. Lead with calm authority. Interfaces should feel premium, deliberate, and trustworthy rather than loud or eager for attention.
4. Favor light, warm, restrained surfaces. Preserve the cream-based palette, careful spacing, and violet accent system as the core visual language.
5. Keep the basics accessible by default. Maintain readable typography, strong enough contrast, clear states, and motion that stays subtle unless there is a strong usability reason otherwise.
